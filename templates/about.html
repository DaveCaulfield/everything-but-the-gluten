Coeliac Disease

Coeliac disease is an autoimmune disorder that occurs in genetically predisposed people where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine.

It is estimated to affect 1 in 100 people worldwide.

The disease is triggered by eating foods containing gluten which then causes the auto immune response attackng the lining of the small intestine.

Gluten is a protein found naturally in wheat, barley, and rye, and is common in foods such as bread, pasta, biscuits and beer.

There is no cure for coeliac disease, the only treatment is a lifelong gluten free diet.
